Good digital accessibility practices for Thierry

Thierry

Accountant at Orange

Grenoble

57 yo

  • Picky
  • Music lover
  • Shy

Motor impairment
Musculoskeletal Disorders (MSDs)

Biography

Thierry is an accountant and a relentless violinist.

He now suffers from serious musculoskeletal disorders that interfere with his daily life, if only to shave or drive. His sedentary professional activity, sitting in front of a computer, only makes things worse.

Frenquently used applications

  • Orange Bank

  • Shazam

Digital uses

For Thierry, the computer is an exclusively professional tool. On average, at work he spends 6 hours a day in front of his PC, so at home he avoids turning it on. In addition, his musculoskeletal disorders make it very painful to use a mouse, even vertical, and navigating from a keyboard remains a sweet dream!

In his private life, the mobile phone is his only digital tool, he uses it for everything: communicate, manage his diary, go shopping, record a concert, take pictures of birds, or activate a metronome. Indispensable to his life, he did not hesitate to invest in a high-performance model.

Équipment

  • Computer : professional laptop PC with large screen, bepo keyboard, vertical mouse and voice recognition software.
  • High-performance smartphone mobile phone
  • Other(s) : Sit-stand desk

Digital disability situations

Drag and drop enforced

Thierry often gets stuck on a site because he can't drag and drop with the mouse.

Difficult complex gestures

On the touch screen of his mobile, Thierry has difficulty performing certain complex gestures.

Voice recognition in public

Thierry cannot use his voice recognition software in the open space, even with his headset, because over time he gets in the way of his colleagues.

Missing click

Thierry often makes accidental clicks and does not always have the possibility of undoing the triggered action.

Daily frustration

  • Suffer from an invisible disability.
  • Being embarrassed to carry out daily actions such as tying one's shoes.
  • Waking up in the middle of the night with tingling.
  • No longer being able to play an entire song.
